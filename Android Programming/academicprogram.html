Academic Program



THE ACADEMIC PROGRAM AIMS TO:


1. make the students recognize God’s existence as a Supreme Being, grow in their faith as Christians and non-Christians, develop a personal relationship with Jesus Christ or Allah, practice Gospel/ Islamic Values, celebrate and sustain life through the sacraments and active prayer life;

2. develop in the students the ability to communicate effectively and responsibly using the English language; and extend literary and information reading in order to appreciate life in its simplicity and understand its sophistication;

3. mahasa ang makabuluhan at makasining na pakikipagtalastasan o pakikipag-ugnayan ng mag-aaral sa kapwa gamit ang mga natutunan sa wika, panitikan at mga napapanahong mga pangyayari upang ipalaganap ang kapayapaan;

4. provide the students the opportunities to find meaning in the exact science of numbers and how they can use this mathematical knowledge in their daily life and in their choice of careers;

5. instill in the students social consciousness, patriotic citizenship, sensitivity to global changes, and determination to share in the sustainable development of the country in this age of technology through responsible use of technology and quality preparation for the world of work;

6. sharpen the scientific minds of the students by engaging them in high order thinking activities such as experimentation, researching and production based on scientific principles to help maintain ecological balance and preservation of God’s creation; and

7. awaken the students’ consciousness to the importance of health and wellness and engage them in the enjoyment of physical activities, the arts and music in all their forms.


THE CURRICULUM:


Notre Dame – RVM College of Cotabato aims to produce excellent graduates who are peace-loving, cultured Filipino, responsive Christians/ non-Christians and Ignacian Marian for others. As a Catholic institution, its curricular offerings provide a holistic Christian/ non-Christian Education that will inculcate in the students Marian qualities deepened with Ignacian Core values of faith, excellence, and service through the use of the RVM Pedagogy wherein the teachers accompany the learners.

The RVM Pedagogy highly emphasizes the use of innovative teaching strategies across subject in the delivery of the curriculum content. It reflects the significance of school’s vision which lies at the heart of all that are designed, implemented, and evaluated. Above all, it makes Christian Living the core of all subjects.

In the spirit of keeping with developments to ensure quality education, the department has taken the bold steps of learning and trying out the framework of Understanding by Design (UbD). The lessons taught were prepared using the best that both RVM Pedagogy and UbD offer. The varied measures of learning assure the teachers of the right steps taken.


The Curricular Offerings:


To provide quality and holistic education to the Marian learners, ND-RVMCC meets the requirements of the Department of Education. In addition, the RVM Education Ministry has guidelines that further enrich the curriculum. The Notre Dame Educational Association provides additional guidelines thus making the curricular program substantial to provide for the realization of the objectives.

Christian Living/ Values Education/ Islamic
The offering is concerned with character formation and witnessing to Gospel values translated to Ignacian and Marian core values of faith, excellence and service.

First Year:
It is about Christian Faith, how God revealed Himself to man out of His love and man’s ultimate response is FAITH.

Second Year
It is Christology which talks about the personality of Jesus, His paschal mystery and the salvation He brought to us and continues to bring to us through the Holy Eucharist.

Third Year
The subject is Christian Morality which teaches the students what is right in what they should think, say and do so they can be called to discipleship.

Fourth Year
The course is on Church and the Sacraments which teaches the students of their being a church and experience belonging to a bigger church, the ecclesial communities with the help of the sacraments.


Values Education

First Year
The focus of the offering is on the Self as Part of the Universe (Man as a Human Being). The lesson emphasizes appreciation of the life and dignity of the human person.

Second Year
The subject focuses on the Self as Part of the Family: A Gift of God (Man as a Social Being). The students are taught the values that promote active participation in the family.

Third Year
The students learn about Self as Part of the Society (Transformative Citizenry for Nation Building). The lesson focuses on Man as a working person who participates in community and national affairs especially in working for the poor and vulnerable.

Fourth Year
The main concern of the subject is the Self in Harmony with the Universe (Man as a Moral Person). The students study how to be in solidarity with and care for God’s creation.


Islamic

First Year
The Islamic Value in focus concerns with the social and spiritual aspects of Holy Qur-an, the daily life of a Muslim, knowledge of Allah and the “Ibaadah”.

Second Year
The focus is on the moral and social domains – the development of social behavior consistent with Islamic teachings. Students learn to make choices, follow school rules and routines, and relate with peers and teachers.

Third Year
The socio-emotional domain is the focus so students increase their awareness and understanding of his relation with Allah, family, community and the bigger world. The development of a personal autonomy results from learning about one’s emotions and dealing with them in appropriate ways.

Fourth Year
The socio-cultural aspect is the focus where the learner understands his culture and relationship with Allah, with other people and the world around him/ her. He learns to think critically, observe, solve problems and understand his identity as Bangsamoro (composed of 13 tribes) and its history.


English with Speech
The course is focused primarily on the development of the English competencies of listening, speaking, reading, writing and viewing which make the students active participants and competent users of the language. The Marians are taught communicative forms and usage for more effective and meaningful communication. The course integrates the fundamentals of grammar with literature with the later serving as a vehicle for values development. The literary selections are chosen for their high interest levels, values and authentic qualities from Philippine Literature for first year; Afro-Asian Literature for second year; British and American Literature for third year; and World Literature for fourth year. Speech classes are given to all students for the needed confidence and effectiveness.

Filipino
Ang Araling Filipino ay humahasa ng mag-aaaral at kaalaman ng mag-aaral sa kahusayan sa pagbasa, pagsasalita, pakikinig, pagsulat at panonood para sa makabuluhang pakikipagtalastasan. Ang araling balarila ay ginagamit upang mapapahalagahan ang mga panitikan na nagtuturo ng mga kaugaliang pangkatauhan (values). Binabasa ang mga maikling kwento, dula, sanaysay, tula, nobela at awit tulad ng Ibong Adarna, Florante at Laura, at ang mga nobelang Noli Me Tangere at El Filibusterismo.

Mathematics
The course offering is specialized for each year level. In first year, it is Elementary Algebra and enriched with Consumers’ Mathematics. In second year, it is Intermediate Algebra made richer with lessons on Pre-Calculus. In third year, the course is Geometry with lessons on Trigonometry. While in fourth year, the course is Advanced Algebra, Statistics and Trigonometry with Calculus topics for enrichment.

Makabayan
This course is a composite of Social Studies, Technology and Home Economics, Computer and Music, Arts, Physical Education and Health.

Social Studies

First Year (Philippine History)
The subject focuses on the national heritage of the Filipinos, its history and its contributions to the world.

Second Year (Asian History)
The focus is on the history of Asian countries, the global changes and contributions to the world.

Third Year (World History)
The course is on the history of nations, their struggles, conflicts and contributions to world peace and development.

Fourth Year (Economics)
The subject focuses on the study of the national patrimony and the economic growth of the country.


Technology and Livelihood Education

First Year – Food and Applied Nutrition, Home and Family Life
The subject teaches food and applied nutrition for the family, home and family life, housing and family economics, home nursing, agricultural arts and entrepreneurship.

Second Year
The offerings of the subject include additional issues of food and applied nutrition, home and family life with basic clothing and clothing construction, handicraft and entrepreneurial competencies.

Third Year
The focus of the subject is on the food and nutrition of people with special needs, baking, the different aspects of industrial arts, handicraft, the basic of electricity and entrepreneurial practices.

Fourth Year
For the final high school offering, the course includes quantitative cookery, foreign dishes and the details of food management and entrepreneurship (feasibility study, business planning, bookkeeping and accounting) and cosmetology.


Computer

First Year
The students are introduced to MS Excel Program using toolbars and other formulas to create Excel documents. The creativity is enhanced with the use of hyperlinks to other programs as E-mail, MS Word and MS Power Point.

Second Year
The subject enriches the use of MS Power Point as an advantageous presentation with the manipulation of the ready made shapes by resizing, rotating, flipping, coloring and combining basic shapes for more complex figures.

Third Year
The Q-Basic Programming is introduced with Computer elements, hardware, software and peopleware. The subject is further enriched with photoshop and computer language.

Fourth Year
The students are given HTML Programming, the tags, sources code writing and program presentation.


Music, Arts, Physical Education and Health

First Year
The subject reinforces nationalism with lessons on Philippine Music, Art and Culture like dances and recreational games. Physical fitness is tested and enhanced with group activities as games and dances. Personality development results from lessons on safety education, hygiene, first aid and drug education.

Second Year
The student is taught to appreciate Asian Music, Art and Culture. Dances and games of Asian origin are experienced in groups/ team efforts. Gymnastics and Aerobics help students to develop physical fitness and well-being. Additional First Aid lessons, other factors to health development and Drug Education complete the offering.

Third Year
European music and arts are the focus of the offering. In Physical Education, the students are taught to be physically fit through varied activities that include sports and foreign dances. The health aspect tackles environmental health, mental health and personality, and additional lesson on drug education.

Fourth Year
The offering provides for music and arts of the 21st century of Latin America, of the Classical and Romantic Period and that of Baroque and Renaissance period. The Physical Education includes Latin American dances and fitness and management of sports like soccer, lawn tennis, baseball and softball. Actual organization and management of a play day is included. For health, the concerns include prevention and treatment of sport injuries, consumer health, responsible parenthood and additional lesson on drug education.


Science and Technology
This course is a specialized offering that covers the fundamentals and concepts of Science and Technology.

First Year – Integrated Science
As the title suggests, the subject covers the basic concepts of the other sciences such as Biology, Chemistry, Physics and Earth Science considering developments in technology.

Second Year – Biology
The subject is considered a life science as it is the study of living creations – man, animals and plants in the midst of the developments in technology.

Third Year – Chemistry
It deals with the study of the properties, composition, and structure of matter considering the practices and developments in technology.

Fourth Year – Physics
The course studies matter and energy and the different principles showing their relationship in the light of technological development.

EXAMINATION POLICIES


Scheduled Examination

The periodical examinations are given four times in one academic year. Four (4) monthly long tests are given in between the periodical examinations. For order in the Cashier’s Officer and the proper conduct of the examinations, the students are expected to observe the following policies:

Only students with EXAMINATION PERMITS ARE ALLOWED TO TAKE THE EXAMINATION. (no permit; no examination)
Promissory notes are accepted provided these are given by the parents/ guardians to the Finance Office one – week before the first day of the exam.
Any student who misses the scheduled examination due to:
unsettled accounts;
sickness or hospitalization that would keep the student away from school;
serious accidents affecting the student or any member of the immediate family; or
death of any member of the family; or
official/ sanctioned participation in contests, trainings or seminars and/ or school representations,
is considered EXCUSED. In such case, the student is entitled to take the SPECIAL EXAMINATION provided that the financial obligations are settled.

Special Examinations are scheduled three (3) school days after the last day of the scheduled examination.
Examination permit is required of any student going to take the Special Examination.
During the examination time, the student is expected to:
observe honesty;
Any student caught cheating in any form gets a 70 in the subject for that examination, in conduct, and in CL/ VE for the quarter.

remain in the classroom except in special cases that the proctors may consider; and
receive the test question to answer and should be submitted at the end of the testing time.
While the teachers do the checking of the test papers, these are returned to the students for post-checking review and clarification.
Examination permits of students without financial obligation for the Quarter are released by the Cashier to the department secretary who turns these over to the Academic Coordinator. From the Academic Coordinator, the Examination permits are given to the year level Chairman who finally gives these to the Class Adviser for distribution to the concerned students.

Examination permits are immediately released as soon as students/ parents/ guardians settle their obligations within the week before the examination.

The CASHIER’S OFFICE IS CLOSED ON THE EXAMINATION DAYS so requests for Examination permit or consideration of promissory notes will not be entertained.
The students are expected to prepare and come on time for the examination days.


Special Periodical Examinations

The Special Periodical Examination is given to the students who missed the scheduled Periodical Examinations. This is given ONCE three (3) days after the last day of scheduled periodical examination. The student who will take the special examination must:

observe ALL the policies for the scheduled Periodical Examination;
get Special Exams Form two (2) days before the exams from the Academic Coordinator and submit the duly accomplished form to the Class Adviser the day before the exams;
pay twenty pesos (P20.00) per subject to be taken to the Finance Office;
be prepared to answer a new set of questions;
understand that ONLY eighty percent (80%) of the total score will be recorded for UNEXCUSED students; and
remember that the SAME REASON for missing the scheduled Periodical Examination can only be used two (2) times, otherwise the Special Examination privilege will be forfeited.

Student representing the school in sanctioned and/ or official activity as contest participation, training, exposure and the like will be treated accordingly.



THE GRADING SYSTEM


The grading system follows the Averaging System where the FINAL GRADE of each subject is the AVERAGE of the grades of the four quarters.

Each subject has required points considering the curricular requirements that the students need to meet. The highest possible grade is 100% and the lowest is 70%.

COMPUTING THE SUBJECT GRADE

The subject grade is based on the components and the corresponding percentages. It is interesting to note that the different subjects have different percentages for the components. This is so because each subject has a unique nature.

In computing the EQUIVALENT, the RAW SCORE shall be divided by the PERFECT SCORE; then multiplied by 50 and added with 50.

Christian Living Quiz example;

Perfect Score = 150
Raw Score = 109
109 ÷ 150 x 50 + 50 = 86.33

The Computed Equivalent (86.33) shall be multiplied by the assigned percentage of the Grade Component of the subject (In this case, Christian Living Quiz is 20%).

CL Quiz Equivalent Example:

86.33 x 0.2 = 17.266 or 17.27
